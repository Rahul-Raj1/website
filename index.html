Who is the bionic arm for?

Bionic arms are worn by people with below-elbow limb differences and upper extremity amputation. We develop bionic limbs that are bespoke to you for optimal comfort and functionality. Whether you have an acquired or congenital limb difference, our team will work with you to ensure that our prototype is the right fit to help you reach your goals.

WHAT MAKES OUR PROTOTYPE BIONIC?

A bionic arm works by picking up signals from a user’s muscles. When a user puts on their bionic arm and flexes muscles in their residual limb just below their elbow; special sensors detect tiny naturally generated electric signals, and convert these into intuitive and proportional bionic hand movement. The bionic hand is controlled by tensing the same muscles that are used to open and close a biological hand.

HAPTIC FEEDBACK

Haptic feedback is a technology that uses vibrations, motions, or forces to simulate the sense of touch, enhancing user interaction with devices. Commonly found in smartphones, gaming controllers, and wearable devices, it provides tactile responses to actions, such as a vibration when typing or pressing a button. This feedback improves user experience by adding a physical dimension to digital interactions.
